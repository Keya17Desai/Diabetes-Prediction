LET'S KNOW ABOUT DIABETES

Diabetes is a metabolic disease resulting in high blood sugar level. Some initial symptoms of diabetes include increased thirst, frequent urination and loss of weight and appetite. If not treated properly, diabetes could have serious complications and may result in failure of vital body organs. The main cause of diabetes is underproduction of insulin in pancreas or the non-responsiveness of body cells to the Insulin. Insulin plays a vital role by instructing the body cells to utilize the glucose that we consume through food. Diabetes may be one of the most misunderstood medical conditions affecting a person's ability to perform their job. This is because there is uncertainty about what Diabetes is and how it should be controlled. After all, there are two primary types of the disease, Type 1 and Type 2. Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus occurs when no insulin is produced resulting in under utilization of glucose by body cells and hence, resulting in a raised Blood Glucose level. Thus Type I diabetic patient needs to externally administer insulin in the body to keep using the glucose. Type I diabetes is rare and it affects only one out of ten diabetic people. Type 2 Diabetes is different from Type 1 and is also the most common of the two. In Type 2 diabetes, the body of the affected produces Insulin, but, the produced insulin fails to utilize the glucose sugar properly.
